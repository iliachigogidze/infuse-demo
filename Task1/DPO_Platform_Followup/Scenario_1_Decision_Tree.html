Scenario 1 — How to Split Vendors to Enforce Company Limit

The DPO admin walks through the rules top to bottom and uses the first one where the condition is met. Splitting prevents vendor collisions — even campaigns without a company limit benefit from separation to avoid duplicate outreach.

Decision Rules — Which Split to Use
1

Split by ABM List

IF campaign has an ABM list
Divide the ABM list into exclusive vendor chunks. Each vendor works only their assigned companies. Recycle idle companies back to the pool after 5 business days. On reassignment, include a suppression list of already-contacted leads.
Admin does Divide the list (even or weighted by vendor capacity / geo sub-goals). Load each chunk into the platform. Set company limit. Brief each vendor with their list and the cap.
Vendor sees "Here's your list of 40 companies. Company limit: 2." Can check remaining slots before calling. New companies may appear over time.
No ABM list ↓
2

Split by Geography

IF campaign GEO has sub-regions (e.g., UK vs Ireland, city-level)
Assign each vendor a geographic territory. Company HQ location determines which vendor owns it.
Admin does Look at GEO sub-goals. Define non-overlapping territories (e.g., Scotland / Northern England / Southern England). Assign one territory per vendor. Brief each vendor with their territory and the cap.
Vendor sees "Your territory: Scotland. Company limit: 3." Thinks this is a Scotland-only campaign.
GEO too broad ↓
3

Split by Industry

IF campaign targets multiple industries
Assign each vendor one or more industry verticals. A company belongs to one industry — no overlap.
Admin does Identify which verticals the campaign covers (explicitly or implicitly). Assign each vendor one or more verticals with enough market to hit their quota. Brief each vendor with their vertical and the cap.
Vendor sees "Target Financial Services. Company limit: 2." Thinks this is a Financial Services campaign.
Industry is "any" ↓
4

Split by Company Size

IF campaign covers a wide company size range
Assign each vendor a size band (e.g., enterprise 5000+, mid-market 500–5000). Employee count or revenue defines the boundary.
Admin does Define non-overlapping size bands with enough companies per band. Assign each vendor a band. Brief each vendor with their band and the cap.
Vendor sees "Target companies 5,000+ employees. Company limit: 2." Thinks this is an enterprise campaign.
Size is "any" ↓
5

No Split — Counter Only

IF company limit ≥ 3 and no dimension available to split
Vendors source companies freely. The slot counter is the only enforcement. Multiple vendors may work the same company — collisions are possible but manageable at higher caps.
Admin does Assign vendors without scope restrictions. Set company limit. Enable pre-check. Monitor collision rates — if too high, consider a mid-campaign retroactive split.
Vendor sees "Any geo, any industry, any size. Company limit: 3." Can check company availability before calling. May find a company blocked if another vendor got there first.
Cap too low for counter alone ↓
6

Stop — Fix Campaign Setup

IF company limit ≤ 2 and no dimension available to split
Collision risk is too high. Campaign does not launch until resolved.
Admin does Flag to campaign manager: "This campaign needs an ABM list or tighter targeting before we go live." Hold the campaign until a split is established. Then re-enter the rules from the top.
Vendor sees Nothing — resolved before the campaign reaches vendors.
Always Running — Underneath All Rules

Per-Company Slot Counter

Every campaign with a company limit has a remaining-slot counter per (campaign + company). Slot consumed on QA approval. Leads in review are reserved. Company closes at 0 slots. In Rules 1–4 this is a safety net. In Rule 5 this is the primary mechanism.

Optional Pre-Check for Vendors

Vendors can look up any company before outreach to see if it's still available and how many slots remain. Not mandatory — vendors can still submit first and find out at submission time. Most valuable in Rule 5 where collisions are possible.

DPO Admin Process — Step by Step
1

Review campaign parameters

Open the campaign initiation document. Note: company limit (if any), ABM list (yes/no), GEO, industry, company size, number of vendors assigned.

2

Determine the split

Walk through Rules 1–6 in order (see above). Use the first rule where the condition is met. If Rule 6 triggers, stop and escalate before proceeding.

3

Configure the platform

Set the company limit so the counter is active. Load vendor-specific scope (company list, territory, vertical, or size band). Enable vendor pre-check.

4

Brief the vendors

Each vendor gets: their targeting scope, the company limit, suppression list (if any), and qualification questions. The brief is written so their slice looks like the whole campaign. Never reference other vendors.

5

Monitor during campaign

Rules 1–4: Review auto-released companies, confirm reassignments, attach suppression lists on reassignment. Rule 5: Watch collision rates — if too high, consider retroactive split. All rules: If a vendor asks why a company is unavailable, answer neutrally: "This company has reached its campaign limit." Never mention other vendors.
